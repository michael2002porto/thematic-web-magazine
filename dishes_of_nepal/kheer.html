Kheer

Khir is a traditional Nepali rice pudding that holds a special place in the hearts and palates of Nepali people. It is a beloved dessert that is prepared and enjoyed on various occasions, from festivals to family gatherings. Khir is not only a delightful treat but also a symbol of Nepali culture and hospitality. To make khir, rice is simmered in milk, often with the addition of sugar, cardamom, and sometimes nuts or raisins for added flavor and texture. The slow cooking process allows the rice to absorb the rich, creamy goodness of the milk, resulting in a luscious, thick pudding with a sweet and aromatic taste. The use of cardamom adds a delightful fragrance to the dish. Khir is commonly prepared during festivals like Dashain, Tihar, and Teej, as well as during special occasions like birthdays and weddings. It is often offered to deities as a religious offering, and then shared with family and guests as a gesture of warmth and generosity. Khir symbolizes the spirit of togetherness, as families and friends gather to enjoy this sweet treat, strengthening their bonds. Nepali khir has regional variations, with some recipes incorporating local ingredients or techniques. Regardless of the variations, khir remains a quintessential Nepali dessert that encapsulates the rich flavors and traditions of Nepal. It is a testament to the importance of food in Nepali culture, not only as sustenance but as a means of expressing love and hospitality.


Ingredients


  • 5 Cups milk, full cream
  • 1/4 cup rice (washed)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 10-12 Raisins
  • 4 Green cardamoms
  • 10-12 almonds (shredded), blanched

Instructions


  1. Boil the rice and milk in a deep pan.
  2. Simmer over low flame, stirring occasionally till the rice is cooked and the milk becomes thick.
  3. When done add sugar, raisins and cardamoms.
  4. Stir till sugar gets dissolved properly.
  5. Transfer into a serving dish and garnish with almonds.
  6. Serve hot or chilled.
