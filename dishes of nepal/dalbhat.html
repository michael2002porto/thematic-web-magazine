Dal Bhat

The daily meal of "Bhat, Dal, and Tarkari" is the cornerstone of Nepali society's culinary traditions and dietary habits. This simple yet nourishing combination reflects the culture, geography, and dietary preferences of the country.
Bhat (Rice): Rice, locally referred to as "Bhat" in Nepal, is a staple in the Nepali diet. It is typically served as the primary source of carbohydrates and energy in every meal. Rice cultivation is a significant agricultural practice in the lowland areas of Nepal, making it readily available and affordable. It is often served as the base on which other dishes are placed.
Dal (Lentil Soup): Dal, or lentil soup, is an essential component of Nepali cuisine. Yellow or red lentils are commonly used, and they are cooked with an array of aromatic spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garlic. Dal provides a valuable source of protein, essential for a balanced diet, especially in regions where meat is less accessible.
Tarkari (Vegetables): Tarkari refers to a variety of cooked vegetables. Nepali cuisine places a strong emphasis on fresh and seasonal vegetables. Tarkari can be prepared in various ways, including stir-frying with spices or as a curry with a tomato-based sauce. The choice of vegetables depends on availability, region, and personal preferences. Common vegetables used in Tarkari include potatoes, cauliflower, spinach, and tomatoes, among others.
The daily consumption of Bhat, Dal, and Tarkari is not only about meeting nutritional needs but also about reflecting the cultural and geographical diversity of Nepal. While rice is a unifying staple, the type of Dal and the selection of Tarkari can vary greatly between regions. For instance, in the Terai (southern plains), people may consume more wheat-based dishes and incorporate different vegetables. In the mountainous regions, buckwheat and barley might replace rice, and root vegetables are more common in Tarkari. This meal also showcases the importance of communal eating in Nepali culture. Families and communities often come together to share Bhat, Dal, and Tarkari, reinforcing bonds and traditions.
In recent years, while the basic components of this meal remain unchanged, some modern variations and global influences have emerged in urban areas of Nepal. However, the traditional Bhat, Dal, and Tarkari continue to be the heart of Nepali cuisine, offering a taste of the country's rich heritage and a reflection of its vibrant cultural diversity.


Ingredients


  • 2 Tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 onion chopped finely
  • 2 cloves of garlic minced
  • 2 " piece of ginger grated
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 cup red lentils washed
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Tablespoons chopped cilantro
  • 2 cups cooked white rice to serve with dal

Instructions


  1. Heat the ghee or oil in a large frying pan and cook the onions until translucent but not brown.
  2. Turn the heat to low and add in the garlic, ginger, crushed coriander seeds, red chili powder and turmeric. Stir to combine for about 3 minutes or so.
  3. Add in the red lentils and mix well with the onion mixture.
  4. Add in the water and bring it to a boil add in the salt and reduce heat to a simmer for about 15 minutes or until the lentils are tender. Stir in the fresh cilantro and remove from heat.
  5. Serve with white boiled or steamed rice
  6. Enjoy
