Sate

Satay is also one of the easiest foods to find in Indonesia. Satay is an Indonesian culinary specialty that uses chicken, beef, goat, rabbit or buffalo meat as its raw material. The meat is cut, marinated and then grilled until cooked. After that, satay is served with various sauces, ranging from peanut sauce or soy sauce. Uniquely, each island has a different version of satay. Starting from Padang Satay from Minangkabau, Madura Satay, Tegal Satay, Klathak Satay, Lilit Satay, Ponorogo Satay, Ambal Satay, and Buntal Satay. What distinguishes each of these satay is the way the meat is selected and the accompanying sauce. Satay is best eaten with white rice or lontong, pickled cucumber and crackers.

Ingredients:

  • Meat (chicken, beef, or lamb)
  • Soy sauce
  • Peanut sauce
  • Skewers

Steps:

  1. Marinate meat with soy sauce.
  2. Skewer and grill until cooked.
  3. Serve with peanut sauce.
