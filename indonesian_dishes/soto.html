Soto

Soto, Coto, Sauto, Tauto is a type of Indonesian soup with chicken or beef stew. Soto usually uses two types of meat, chicken and beef. Chicken soto has a yellowish broth with a savory and fragrant taste. The yellow color is due to turmeric, shallots, garlic as the basic spices. While meat soto is usually clear in color or coconut milk. Soto can be found in every region, especially on the island of Java. There are many variants of soto in Indonesia, one of which is Soto Lamongan, Soto Kudus, Soto Betawi, Soto Bandung, Soto Padang, Soto Jepara, Soto Banjar, Coto Makasar, etc. Soto is served with complements that vary from region to region. Soto Bandung is served with radish and fried soy beans, Soto Kudus is served with boiled bean sprouts, Soto Lamongan is served with sliced cabbage, tomatoes and koya, etc. Soto is best enjoyed warm, especially on rainy days or when you're not feeling well. The protein and various spices contained in a bowl of soto can restore stamina and warm the body.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken or beef
  • Rice noodles
  • Broth
  • Lime
  • Fried garlic
  • Boiled egg

Steps:

  1. Boil meat and noodles.
  2. Serve in broth with toppings.
